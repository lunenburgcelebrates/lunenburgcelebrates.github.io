June 7 – 12, 2016

Join in celebrating Lunenburg’s 263rd birthday!

Once again this year, various groups and organizations are hosting events to help mark the Town’s 263rd birthday and local residents are encouraged to take some time to enjoy all that our beautiful town has to offer. The actual birthday is June 7 but most activities will take place the following weekend.

Connect with us on #lunenburgcelebrates

Tuesday, June 7

Bluenose Academy is celebrating the Town’s birthday with a “Unity Day”. Students will take part in African Dancing workshops and a Heritage Scavenger Hunt around “Old Town” Lunenburg. The Mayor will address the students in the afternoon.

Knaut-Rhuland House opens the 2016 season from 11:00am – 5:00pm.

Friday, June 10

Bluenose Coastal Action Foundation will once again host their very popular “Rum and Chowder Social” fundraiser but please note that the venue has changed and this year’s event will be held at the Lunenburg Community Centre from 6:00 – 9:00pm. Sample some of our local chef’s best chowders and wash it down with a variety of rum choices.

Tickets are $30.00 in advance or $35.00 at the door. For tickets contact BCAF at 634-9977.

Lunenburg School of the Arts Exhibition and Artist Talk “Hot Diggity Dog” with LSA’s Artist-in-Residence Melanie Doiron in the East Bay ceramics room at 7:30pm.

Saturday, June 11

The Fisheries Museum of the Atlantic Open House Weekend. Free admission for everyone with lots of new exhibits on display both inside the museum and on the wharf. Hours are 9:30am – 5:00pm. The Museum will also be sponsoring wharf side entertainment throughout the weekend.

The Dory Shop will be open for drop in tours. Times TBA.

Trot In Time buggy tours will be running all day. Stop by and fill out a ballot for a free Family Package buggy tour for two adults and two children.

Lunenburg Board of Trade opens the wharf Visitor Information Centre, hours are 10:00am – 6:00pm.

Bluenose Coastal Action Foundation will have an information booth set up on the museum wharf and will offer an educational session on Endangered White Fish by Andrew Breen at 2:00pm in the Fisheries Museum.

Iron Works Distillery will be open from 11:00am – 7:00pm and will offer three free tours as follows: 12:00pm, 2:00pm and 4:00pm with a maximum of 14 persons per tour.

Knaut-Rhuland House Open House Weekend: 11:00am – 5:00pm with gifts offered to those who identify themselves as descendants of Lunenburg’s founding families.

Pleasant Paddling in Blue Rocks is offering 50% off kayak rentals.

The Canadian Dory Rowing Association will offer “Try It” sessions from 10:00am – 12:00pm from their headquarters on the wharf adjacent to the boat tour shed.

Specialty Pop Corn will be available on the wharf throughout the day.

Restaurants and Retail shops will have specials on over the weekend. Look for “Be A Tourist” posters advertising these savings.

Blue Dream Schooner Project will be open for public viewing at the Boat Shed during the afternoon. Times TBA.

South Shore Regional Library Lego Heritage Project finalists will have their creations on display at the Fisheries Museum throughout the day. They will also have a booth set up on the wharf to provide information and dispense library cards.

Churches open for visitation:

  • St. Norbert’s Roman Catholic Church, 1:00 – 4:00pm
  • Central United Church, 10:00am – 4:00pm
  • St. John’s Anglican Church, 10:00am – 4:00pm

Boscowan Inn is offering a 20% reduction for one night’s accommodation for Lunenburg and area residents. A Happy Hour is also planned for late Saturday afternoon and early evening.

New Voice Academy will host an open house from 1:00 – 4:00pm.

Bluenose II will be in port and open for wharfside tours.

LAMP Evening Concert – Ocean Inspired @ LAMP from 5:00 – 7:00pm with poet Sue Goyette offering selected readings accompanied by ocean inspired music and cocktails created with premium spirits from Iron Works Distillery and Petitie Riviere Winery. Cost is $50.00 which includes admission to Sunday’s concert of World Premieres.

Lunenburg School of the Arts Open House with fun activities for all from 12:00 – 4:00pm.

Halifax and Southwestern Railway Museum and Model Railroad open with free admission from 10:00am – 5:00pm

Lorne Street Fire Company of Lunenburg open for free tours from 10:00 am – 2:00pm

Sunday, June 12

The Fisheries Museum of the Atlantic Open House Weekend continues with lots of activities for the whole family throughout the day from 9:30am – 5:00pm.

Annual 5km Muffin Run begins at 9:30am from the Lunenburg Community Centre parking lot. Between 400 and 500 runners usually show up for this very popular race through Lunenburg and the Back Harbour highway. Come out to cheer them on.

Knaut-Rhuland House Open House continues from 11:00am – 5:00pm with gifts for descendants of founding families.

Lunenburg’s Birthday Cake served at 1:00pm at the Fisheries museum.

South Shore Genealogical Society invites everyone to their Open House at Room 203, Lunenburg Academy from 1:00 – 4:00pm. Ribbon Cutting to officially open their new premises at 2:00pm.

Trot In Time buggy tours continue throughout the day. Remember to fill out your ballot for a free family buggy tour.

Lunenburg Walking Tours will offer two free tours – the Essential Lunenburg tour begins at 2:00pm at Lunenburg Academy grounds and the Haunted Lunenburg tour begins at 8:30pm at the Mariner King Inn on King Street.

LAMP Composition Academy Showcase at 2:30pm. Enjoy an afternoon of world premieres and a diverse selection of musicians as LAMP presents all new works created by five composers selected for its 2016 Composition Academy. Tickets $30.

Churches open for visitation:

  • St. Norbert’s Roman Catholic Church, 1:00 – 4:00pm
  • St. John’s Anglican Church , 10:00am – 4:00pm

Halifax and Southwestern Railway Museum and Model Railroad open with free admission from 1:00pm – 5:00pm
